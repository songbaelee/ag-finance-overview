Market Sizing

How big is the agri-SME financing gap in Sub-Saharan Africa, and who is currently meeting it? The figures below come from a 2022 sector study; below that, we've built a simple illustrative breakdown to make the shape of the gap easier to picture.

Source: CASA / ISF Advisors, “State of the Agri-SME Sector: Bridging the Finance Gap” (2022). Read the full report (PDF)

What the report tells us

~130,000 Agri-SMEs in Sub-Saharan Africa
~$88.1B Total financing demand
$74B Annual financing gap
~$14B Formal supply currently met
~16% Share of demand met

Our illustrative modeling Illustrative

The report doesn't break the agri-SME market down by firm size or by who is supplying capital. To help make the size and shape of the gap concrete, we built an illustrative segmentation below. It is CRS/Claude modeling, not data from the report — treat it as a way to think about the market's structure, not a precise measurement.

Modeling notes & assumptions
  • No "micro" tier — micro/smallholder firms (~33M informal smallholder farms) are a different population, not part of the report's 130,000 agri-SME count.
  • DFIs excluded as a capital-provider category — they lend to large corporates, not true SMEs.
  • Value chain/off-taker finance excluded from this segmentation for now — kept simple as banks vs. investment funds only.
  • Guarantee-backed lending and government/donor schemes folded into "banks" (treated as a channel, not a distinct capital source).
  • Banks assumed concentrated in the upper SME tier (collateral/track record barriers exclude smaller firms); investment funds assumed capital-constrained overall but reach further down-market proportionally.
